In English

Beginners' course starts on Sunday 15.9.2013, but it is possible to join until 29th of September. Practices are on Tuesdays at 8.00-9.15, on Thursdays at 8.00-9.00 and on Sundays at 19.45-21.15 at the Administration Building sport facilities. In the beginners' course you will only need a t-shirt and tracksuit bottoms, shinai can be borrowed from the club. Kendo beginners' course lasts approximately three months and costs 60 euros. Unisport training fees are not required.

Kendo is a modern Japanese martial art with roots in the traditional swordmanship of the feudal samurai. It reached its contemporary form in the 20th century, during which it also began to spread outside of Japan. In Finland kendo has been practiced since 1987.

Kendo is practiced with a flexible bamboo sword called shinai and the strikes and thrusts are aimed at specific target areas covered by a protective armour. Kendo as a sport requires technical skill rather than muscles and regular practice improves physical fitness, coordination and concentration.

Beginners of kendo can find the manner of practice most suited for them through regular training and continue to review it as they progress as well as get older. This makes it possible for people of all ages to start practicing kendo, from elementary school children to adults. A 60-year-old kendoka, practitioner of kendo, is no unusual sight at the training. Indeed, kendo is well suited for a lifelong activity.
